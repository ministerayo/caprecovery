FAQs

Is Crypto A Good Investment?

Crypto assets are the latest asset class and are highly volatile. Since these assets are highly volatile, the chances of their value appreciating or depreciating more than 1000X are high. 

The crypto market is unregulated; thus, it is a risky market. If you lose money, your chances of recovering it depend on how quickly you act and what steps you take. Another threat is that crypto scams are quite common.

How To Avoid Cryptocurrency Scams?

Con artists are smart, and they will work on your emotions to get what they want. They will entice their victims by offering fake deals.

  • Once-in-a-lifetime opportunity
  • Exaggerated and unbelievable returns on investments
  • Get quick schemes
  • Low risk and high returns on investments.
  • Their firm is so expert at picking cryptos at the perfect time that they rarely have any losses.
  • Ponzi schemes are common in crypto scams.
How To Spot A Cryptocurrency Scammer?

Remember that scammers seek you out to make quick money. All scammers follow the following strategies to entice their victims:

  • Offering unrealistic returns on investment
  • Instilling fear and greed in your mind
  • A small window of opportunity to make a substantial amount of money
  • Employing high-pressure sales strategies
  • Using coercive language to convince you
  • They show a remarkable need to close the deal as quickly as possible.
  • They will also prompt you to apply for loans to invest in these bogus schemes.
How can I Be Sure That I am Dealing With A Trusted Crypto Broker?

While selecting a genuine and reputed broker, here are a few things that you need to research to avoid falling prey to an online cryptocurrency scam.

  • They have their license and registration from a government agency or a reputed and trusted private entity.
  • Check if these certificates are valid.
  • These certificates should be mentioned on their website.
  • They must not be under any litigation.
  • They must not be blacklisted.
  • Check their reviews; it will help you understand how they operate and how their client service center helps with questions and queries.
What Reporting Steps To Follow If You Get Crypto Scammed?

When you realize you have been scammed, you need to quickly take action and take precautions to minimize the losses further.

  • Refrain from interacting with the scammers any further.
  • Make a report and include all the details from the time the scammer contacted you until the time you realized that you were a victim of it.
  • Contact the police, your bank, and legal authorities to report the scammer.
  • Connect with the FBI’s Internet Crime Complaint Center and also file a complaint with them.
  • Request a free annual credit report and check for any suspicious activities.
  • Contact the FTC, or Federal Trade Commission, and ask them about the scam.
